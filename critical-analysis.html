Critical Analysis of Section 115BAA- Tax on certain domestic companies

  • 01. This is applicable from FY 2019-20 (AY 2020-201) onwards
  • 02. This can be opted for any assessment year on or after AY 2020-2021
  • 03. There is NO TIME LIMIT for opting the scheme
  • 04. Once the option is exercised by the tax payer, it cannot be subsequently withdrawn
  • 05. Option should be exercised by filing form 10-IC on or before the due date of filing of ITR
  • 06. Base Rate of Tax under this section will be 22%
  • 07. Conditions for availing the benefit under this section:

  1. Deductions under section 10A, Section 32(1)(iia), Section 32AD, Section 33AB, Section 33ABA, Certain Clauses of Section 35, Section 35AD, Section 35CCC, Section 35CCD or any other deduction under Chapter VIA under heading C (except section 80JJAA) are NOT ALLOWED.
    Remarks: Above given deductions are industry specific so any such deduction that falls under section 36 to 43B (which are general sections for deductions in most of the Companies) are ALLOWABLE under this new option as well.
    So, there is no change under this clause for companies in general business (not claiming any specific deductions as mentioned above).

  2. Set off of any loss carried forward or depreciation from any earlier assessment year, if such loss or depreciation is attributable to any deductions in clause referred above is NOT ALLOWED.
    Remarks: If the entity has carried forward losses attributable to any deductions referred in first clause then such losses/depreciation will not be allowed to set off. All other business losses and Depreciation carried forward from earlier assessment years will be ALLOWED.
    From the perspective of a normal business entity wherein special deduction sections are not applicable, there is no change in setting off the carry forward losses or unabsorbed depreciation.

  3. Set off of any loss or allowance of unabsorbed depreciation deemed so under section 72A, if such loss or depreciation is attributable to any deductions in clause referred above is NOT ALLOWED.
    Remarks: No impact as such as again it relates to attributable loss/depreciation to deductions referred in first clause.

  4. Claim of Depreciation, if any, under any provisions of section 32, except Depreciation mentioned u/s 32(1)(iia) is ALLOWED.
    Remarks: To make it simple, any claim of depreciation {other than additional depreciation as mentioned u/s 32(1)(iia)} is ALLOWABLE. Since there is no time limit for adopting this section so Companies having claim on account of additional depreciation are advised to take claim and opt for this section in subsequent years.

  5. Provisions of section 115JB relating to MAT itself shall not be applicable to the domestic company which exercises option under section 115BAA
    Remarks: Companies need to analyse their business for forgoing the lower tax benefit under MAT.

  6. Tax credit of MAT paid by the domestic company exercising option under section 115BAA of the Act shall not be available consequent to exercising of such option.
    Remarks: As there is no time line within which option under section 115BAA can be exercised, it may be noted that a domestic company having credit of MAT may. if it so desires, exercise the option after utilising the said credit against the regular tax payable under the current taxation regime and later on opt for new regime under this section.
